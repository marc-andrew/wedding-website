Stephansplatz & Stephansdom

City Centre, Historical, Church, Café, Shopping

The Stephansplatz is one of the most important squares in the city centre of Vienna with street performers, horse-drawn carriages, shops and coffee places.

The area on Stephansplatz around the cathedral was originally a graveyard – making it the dead centre of Vienna in a very literal sense. But with plague and influenza epidemics striking Europe in the 1730s, Karl VI ordered the graveyard to be closed and henceforth Vienna buried its dead beneath Stephansdom in the ‘New Tombs’, which in the 19th century became more wistfully known as Katakomben (catacombs).

The Stephansdom, also known for St. Stephen's Cathedral, is Vienna’s Gothic masterpiece and pride. From outside you will see the glorious tiled roof, with its dazzling row of chevrons and Austrian eagle. Inside, the magnificent Gothic stone pulpit presides over the main nave, fashioned in 1515 by Anton Pilgrim.

If you have time, go and see the gigantic view across Vienna from the tollest south tower at 136.44 meters, is reached via 343 steps.

Staatsoper

City Centre, Opera House

The Staatsoper is Vienna's World-famous opera house. Built between 1861 and 1869 by August Siccardsburg and Eduard van der Nüll, the Staatsoper initially appalled the Viennese public and Habsburg royalty. It was originally called the Vienna Court Opera.

Schönbrunn Palace

Museum, Park, Zoo

The Habsburgs' overwhelmingly opulent summer palace is now a Unesco World Heritage site. Of the palace's 1441 rooms, 40 are open to the public; the Imperial Tour takes you into 26 of these, including the private apartments of Franz Josef and Sisi, while the Grand Tour covers all 40 and includes the precious 18th-century interiors from the time of Maria Theresia.

If you have limited time and you don't want to see the palace from inside, walk up the hill and see the view from the top, maybe have a coffee in the beautiful café the Glorietta.

The worlds oldest Zoo is in Schönbrunn which was once a private menagerie of emperor Franz Stephan and Maria Theresa.

Fancy a swim? I would recommend you go to the Schoenbrunnerbad: https://www.schoenbrunnerbad.at/

National Library

Museum, Culture

The Austrian National Library (German: Österreichische Nationalbibliothek) is the largest library in Austria, with more than 12 million items in its various collections. The library is located in the Neue Burg Wing of the Hofburg in center of Vienna and it looks like in beauty and the beast. The place in front of the National library is called "heldenplatz" - the hero's square. It is also where Hitler announced the "Anschluss" or annexation of Austria unfortunately... :( Apparently so few people showed up to Hitlers speech that they filmed it from a corner angle, to create the illusion of a filled square.

Natural History Museum

Museum

The Naturhistorisches Museum covers four billion years of natural history in a blink. With its exquisitely stuccoed, frescoed halls and eye-catching cupola, this late-19th-century building is the identical twin of the Kunsthistorisches Museum opposite.

Among its minerals, fossils and dinosaur bones are one-of-a-kind finds like the minuscule 25,000-year-old Venus von Willendorf and a peerless 1100-piece meteorite collection. Panoramic rooftop tours take you onto the building's roof to view the ornate architecture up-close.

We do recommend popping by the Natural History Museum.

Kunsthistorisches Museum

Museum

The Kunsthistorisches Museum is an art museum and it is housed in its festive palatial building on Ringstrasse, it is crowned with an octagonal dome.

The term Kunsthistorisches Museum applies to both the institution and the main building. It is the largest art museum in the country and it takes you on a time-travel treasure hunt from Classical Rome to Egypt and the Renaissance.

If your time's limited, skip straight to the Picture Gallery, where you'll want to dedicate at least an hour or two to Old Masters.

MuseumsQuartier

Museum, Café, Hang Out

The MuseumsQuartier is a remarkable ensemble of museums, cafes, restaurants and bars inside former imperial stables designed by Fischer von Erlach.

This breeding ground of Viennese cultural life is the perfect place to hang out and watch or meet people on warm evenings. With over 90,000 sq metres of exhibition space – including the Leopold Museum, MUMOK, Kunsthalle, Architekturzentrum and Zoom – the complex is one of the world’s most ambitious cultural hubs.

Schloss Belvedere

Palace, Garden

A masterpiece of total art, Schloss Belvedere is one of the world’s finest baroque palaces. Designed by Johann Lukas von Hildebrandt (1668–1745), it was built for the brilliant military strategist Prince Eugene of Savoy, conqueror of the Turks in 1718. What giddy romance is evoked in its sumptuously frescoed halls, replete with artworks by Klimt, Schiele and Kokoschka.
