About Vienna

Airport Transport

No need to take a taxi, there is a Schnell Bahn/ S-Bahn (train) which will only cost you € 4.20 to get to the city centre. Just follow the signs at the airport for the S-Bahn.

There is a quick train option called the CAT (City Airport Train) which will bring you to Landstrasse in 15 minutes and will cost you €29 return. Visit https://www.cityairporttrain.com/ for more information and time tables.

Public Transport

In Vienna you can jump on any Wiener Linien services (U-Bahn, trams and buses), there are no barriers like in London, Paris, New York or anywhere else. The Austrians trust you will buy a ticket but they will do random checks, so it is better to have a ticket. You can buy your ticket at U-Bahn stations and on trams in a Tabakladen (Trafik; tobacco kiosk), as well as from a few staffed ticket offices.

If you are staying in the centre, then we would recommend you walk the city, it is the best way to see all the lovely streets and shops you usually wouldn't.

Tickets cover all public transport. If you need a ticket you can go for the following options:

  • Single Ticket (Einzelfahrschein) good for one journey with line changes. It costs €2.40 and €2.60 if purchased on trams.
  • 24-hour Ticket (24-Stundenkarten) €8, you will need to validate this ticket before getting on.
  • 48-hour Ticket (48-Stundenkarten) €14.10, you will need to validate this ticket before getting on.
  • 72-hour Ticket (72-Stundenkarten) €17.10, you will need to validate this ticket before getting on.
  • Weekly Ticket (Wochenkarte) €17.10, valid Monday to Sunday only, ie tickets purchased on a Friday are still only valid to the Sunday.

For all ticket options please visit https://shop.wienerlinien.at/index.php/tickets.

For more information how to use a ticket please visit http://www.visitingvienna.com/transport/public/.

U-Bahn

The U-Bahn is a quick, efficient and inexpensive way of getting around the city. There are five lines: U1, U2, U3 U4 and U6 (there is currently no U5). Platforms have timetable information and signs showing the exits and nearby facilities. The U-Bahn runs from 5am to midnight Monday to Thursday and continuously from Friday through to Sunday night.

You can find the U-Bahn map here.

Tram

The tram is the perfect way to view the city on the cheap. Trams are either numbered or lettered (eg 1, 2, D) and cover the city centre and some suburbs. Services run from 5.15am to 11.45pm.

Buses

Each bus stops has maps of all the stops and there destinations. Many bus stops now have a countdown indicator board which, as the name Bus name and destination, counts down the arrival of the buses so you will know how long you will have to wait. These are usually pretty accurate, traffic allowing. Remember, before hopping on you will need a valid ticket.

Night Buses

Night buses (Nightline) routes cover much of the city and run every half-hour from 12.30am to 5am. Note that on early Saturday and Sunday mornings (ie after midnight Friday and Saturday) the U-Bahn runs all night. Schwedenplatz, Schottentor and Kärntner Ring/Oper are stopping points for many night bus services; look for buses and bus stops marked with an 'N'. All transport tickets are valid for Nightline services.

Bicycle

Vienna is a fabulous place to get around by bike. Bicycles can be carried free of charge on carriages marked with a bike symbol on the U-Bahn from 9am to 3pm and after 6.30pm Monday to Friday, after 9am Saturday and all day Sunday. It's not possible to take bikes on trams or buses.

The city also runs the Citybike Wien shared-bike program, with bike stands scattered throughout the city. It's free for the first hour and €1 for the secound hour. A €1 registration fee is required before using the Citybike services.

Drinking Water

In Vienna we have high quality tap water as good as any bottled mineral water, you will only realise how bad other countries tap water is once you have been to Vienna :)

Over 95% over tap water in Vienna comes from the alps so no need to buy mineral water from the supermarket, just fill up your reuseable bottle with tap water before leaving the house.

Adaptor

In Austria we have a 2-pin system (Type C, Type F) as is common almost all over Europe, if you are coming from the UK you will need an adaptor.

Paying by Card

You can usually pay by card but don't assume you can because there are many places that don't take cards. Always carry cash with you to be on the safe side.

Tipping

Tip is never included in bills, you will usually add it on top. When tipping you usually round up or tip between 10-15%. When you tip you need to tell the waiter how much you want to tip them or leave the tip amount on the table.

Opening times for Stores

During the week shops are usually open from 8am-8pm, on Saturdays they close earlier at 6pm and on Sundays all shops are closed except for an occasional supermarket at some stations.

Going to a Restaurant

Don't click, wave or shout at your waiter to get attention, it's the fast track to them disappearing for 20 minutes.

Generally, you will have table service in most establishments. Very, very rarely will you need to order at the bar/counter.

The best way to ask for help is to say 'entschuldigung' and for the bill is 'Zahlung bitte'.

Be as polite and friendly as possible, even if they are 'rude' in the beginning, Viennese waiters warm up once you settle in and can even start to banter with you, if you have the right (not overly entitled) attitude.

Smoking

Yes, smoking is still a thing in Vienna, unfortunately. Most places have smoking, and non-smoking areas and you may have to walk through the smoking area to get to the non-smoking area.

Here's the thing though – if you refuse to go into any smoky bar or restaurant, you are going to miss half the city. So be prepared to have a shower when you get back home if you do go to a place that allows smoking.
